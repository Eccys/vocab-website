chiaroscuro

noun | kee-ah-roh-SKYOOR-oh

What It Means

Chiaroscuro is a visual effect created by strong contrasts between light and dark.

// The artist used chiaroscuro to add drama to his painting.

chiaroscuro in context

Used in art, photography, and literature to describe dramatic contrasts.

Did You Know?

This term comes from Italian and literally means 'light-dark.' It was popularized by Renaissance artists like Caravaggio and Rembrandt to create dramatic depth in paintings.
